## DOMAIN: Commitment to Pupils and Pupil Learning

### Competency: Teachers demonstrate commitment to the well-being and development of all pupils.

Mr. Den Haan demonstrates a strong commitment to the well-being and development of all students in his role as STAC teacher. He creates a welcoming and positive environment where students feel valued and respected from the moment they enter the space. His practice of greeting students by name, celebrating birthdays with guitar and song, and taking time to know each learner personally fosters a sense of belonging that extends beyond the classroom walls. Mr. Den Haan understands that students who feel welcome and respected achieve more positive results, and he intentionally builds rapport through positive interactions during supervision duties and within the STAC environment.

He supports student development by providing opportunities for creative expression through hands-on projects such as cardboard house building, arcade game development, and mechanical arm construction. These experiences allow students to develop practical skills while building confidence and self-efficacy. Mr. Den Haan's commitment to equity is evident in his efforts to ensure all students feel represented and valued, regardless of their prior experience with technology or hands-on learning.

### Competency: Teachers are dedicated in their efforts to teach and support pupil learning and achievement.

Mr. Den Haan is dedicated to supporting pupil learning and achievement through structured, skill-building experiences in the STAC. He provides guided opportunities for students to develop competence with hand and machine tools, ensuring they have the support needed to succeed while also building independence. He shares clear safety videos, models proper tool usage, and provides ongoing supervision and constructive feedback as students develop their skills.

His teaching philosophy emphasizes that "anything worth doing is worth doing poorly"—not to encourage failure, but to foster perseverance and a growth mindset. Mr. Den Haan gives students room to learn from mistakes while providing the scaffolding necessary for eventual success. He balances direct instruction with student autonomy, allowing learners to troubleshoot obstacles and develop problem-solving skills that will serve them beyond the STAC environment.

### Competency: Teachers treat all pupils equitably and with respect.

Mr. Den Haan treats all students equitably and with respect, ensuring that every learner who enters the STAC feels welcome and valued. In the limited time he has with each rotary class, he makes deliberate efforts to touch base with every student, whether they are working on projects, conducting research, or using the space for other assignments. He maintains positive interactions with students across all grades, greeting them warmly during morning supervision and throughout the school day.

He understands that some students may struggle with the academic demands of other subjects, and he uses the STAC as a space where these learners can experience success and build confidence. Mr. Den Haan's commitment to fairness is evident in his efforts to include students who may find themselves working alone despite wanting to collaborate, and in his willingness to provide extra assistance to those who need it. His calm, firm approach to classroom management, combined with positive rapport, creates an environment where all students can thrive.

### Competency: Teachers provide an environment for learning that encourages pupils to be problem-solvers, decision-makers, lifelong-learners, and contributing members of a changing society.

Mr. Den Haan provides a learning environment that encourages students to become problem-solvers, decision-makers, and lifelong learners through hands-on, inquiry-based experiences. He introduces students to the joys of working with tools and materials, encouraging them to develop skills they may not have encountered before and to consider future pathways in technical and creative fields. Many students enter the STAC without prior shop experience, and Mr. Den Haan guides them in exploring whether they wish to further develop these capabilities.

He fosters problem-solving by providing guidance and feedback when students encounter obstacles in their creative visions, rather than solving problems for them. Projects such as the cardboard house challenge—where students must build structures to withstand the "little bad wolf" (human breath) and the "big bad wolf" (leaf blower)—require critical thinking, experimentation, and iteration. These experiences help students understand that great outcomes come through perseverance and continued practice, preparing them to be adaptable contributors to a changing society.

## DOMAIN: Professional Knowledge

### Competency: Teachers know their subject matter, the Ontario curriculum, and education-related legislation.

Mr. Den Haan demonstrates strong subject matter knowledge in the areas of tool use, safety protocols, and applied technological concepts. He draws on his extensive experience with hand and machine tools to assist students with their building projects, troubleshooting challenges and guiding them toward successful outcomes. His understanding of safety legislation and board policies is evident in his constant emphasis on safe practices, which he discusses regularly with students to ensure their well-being and that of their peers.

He aligns his instruction with curriculum expectations across multiple subject areas, integrating mathematics through measurement, science through structural engineering concepts, and literacy through research and planning activities. His knowledge of the Grade 3 curriculum is evident in his support for students preparing for EQAO examinations through keyboarding practice and technology integration. Mr. Den Haan's ability to make cross-curricular connections enhances student understanding and demonstrates his mastery of subject knowledge.

### Competency: Teachers know a variety of effective teaching and assessment practices.

Mr. Den Haan employs effective teaching and assessment practices that are well-suited to the STAC environment. He recognizes that students' first experiences with shop tools can result in varied outcomes and adapts his instruction accordingly, helping learners identify obstacles to success and chart paths to desired outcomes. His assessment approach includes observation during guided practice, constructive feedback as students develop competence, and gradual release of responsibility as confidence grows.

He balances formative assessment—through demonstrations, guided practice, and ongoing feedback—with summative evaluation of completed projects. Mr. Den Haan uses assessment to inform instruction, modifying his approach based on student readiness and needs. His philosophy of allowing students room to succeed while learning tenacity aligns with research-informed practices that support student autonomy and resilience. He celebrates growth and effort, recognizing that confidence and joy in creating are as important as technical proficiency.

### Competency: Teachers know a variety of effective classroom management strategies.

Mr. Den Haan employs effective classroom management strategies that prioritize safety, positive rapport, and clear expectations. In a space where students work with potentially dangerous tools, he establishes and reinforces boundaries through positive relationships rather than punitive measures. He explains expectations clearly and models the calm, focused behaviour he expects from students.

When conflicts arise, Mr. Den Haan uses learned conflict resolution techniques to defuse situations rather than escalate them. His approach includes using a firm but calm voice, engaging in discrete discussions with students to redirect them toward learning goals, and maintaining consistent safety protocols. His management style reflects an understanding that students who feel respected and understood are more likely to meet behavioural expectations, creating a productive and safe learning environment for all.

### Competency: Teachers know how pupils learn and factors that influence pupil learning and achievement.

Mr. Den Haan demonstrates understanding of how students learn and the factors that influence their achievement in the STAC environment. He recognizes that students have different comfort levels with hands-on learning and group work, and he adapts his approach accordingly. While he encourages collaboration through group projects such as the UNSDG assignment and cardboard house building, he respects individual learners who prefer to work independently, providing them with additional support.

He provides multiple access points for learning, including video demonstrations, live modeling, and hands-on practice. Mr. Den Haan understands that motivation varies among students and seeks ways to engage those who may be less productive, consulting colleagues for strategies to encourage participation. His awareness of the social dynamics of group work—particularly his attention to students who find themselves working alone despite wanting to collaborate—demonstrates his commitment to ensuring all learners have positive, productive experiences.

## DOMAIN: Teaching Practice

### Competency: Teachers use their professional knowledge and understanding of pupils, curriculum, legislation, teaching practices, and classroom management strategies to promote the learning and achievement of their pupils.

Mr. Den Haan uses his professional knowledge to promote student learning and achievement through carefully structured, purposeful instruction in the STAC. He develops clear expectations for tool use and project completion, modeling skills and techniques through demonstrations that students then replicate. His instructional time is focused and purposeful, with attention to measurement accuracy, safety protocols, and the creative process.

He organizes learning around meaningful projects that allow students to apply their developing skills, from following detailed instructions for mechanical arm construction to modifying plans for cardboard house building. Mr. Den Haan assists students in developing ways to access and critically assess information through research activities using Chromebooks, supporting their growth as independent learners. His integration of the UNSDG project demonstrates his ability to connect STAC learning to global citizenship and cross-curricular expectations.

### Competency: Teachers communicate effectively with pupils, parents, and colleagues.

Mr. Den Haan communicates effectively with students, colleagues, and families to support learning in the STAC. With students, he provides clear instructions through multiple modalities—verbal explanations, video demonstrations, and hands-on modeling—ensuring that learners understand expectations and safety protocols. His positive, professional attitude fosters open communication and encourages students to ask questions and seek help when needed.

With colleagues, he communicates regularly regarding student progress, sharing examples of student success and offering solutions to behavioural concerns. His use of Google Classroom to provide video links and project details extends communication beyond the STAC, supporting students and classroom teachers in understanding project requirements. Mr. Den Haan's communication with families is evidenced through his participation in school-wide initiatives and his role in organizing field experiences that connect classroom learning to community opportunities.

### Competency: Teachers conduct ongoing assessment of their pupils' progress, evaluate their achievement, and report results to pupils and parents regularly.

Mr. Den Haan conducts ongoing assessment of student progress in the STAC, using observation, demonstration, and feedback to guide learning. When students first use machine tools such as scroll saws and belt sanders, he provides close supervision, constructive criticism, and answers to their questions. As students become more comfortable and successful, he shifts to providing positive feedback that builds confidence and reinforces safe, effective practices.

His assessment approach includes observation of students during guided practice, evaluation of project outcomes, and feedback conversations that help learners understand their growth areas. While the rotary schedule presents challenges for ongoing reporting, Mr. Den Haan ensures students receive timely feedback during their STAC time. His documentation of student progress and his communication with classroom teachers regarding student achievement support a comprehensive understanding of each learner's development.

### Competency: Teachers adapt and refine their teaching practices through continuous learning and reflection, using a variety of sources and resources.

Mr. Den Haan demonstrates commitment to continuous learning and reflection in his teaching practice. He regularly engages students in dialogue about future employment possibilities using the technology available in the STAC, helping them understand how the skills they are developing can translate to high school programs and career pathways. For students who struggle academically, he intentionally enlightens them to opportunities that await in technical fields, broadening their understanding of post-secondary possibilities.

He seeks input from colleagues regarding strategies to engage students who may be less motivated to take advantage of STAC learning opportunities, demonstrating openness to feedback and a willingness to refine his practice. His participation in professional learning opportunities—including workshops on technology integration, STEAM education, and student engagement—reflects his commitment to staying current with educational trends and applying new learning to enhance student experiences.

### Competency: Teachers use appropriate technology in their teaching practices and related professional responsibilities.

Mr. Den Haan uses appropriate technology to enhance student learning and improve the efficiency of his teaching practice. In the STAC, he demonstrates effective use of measurement, cutting, and finishing tools to help students realize their creative visions. He also integrates digital technology through the use of Chromebooks for research activities, such as the UNSDG project for Grades 6-8, and for supporting Grade 3 students in developing keyboarding skills in preparation for EQAO examinations.

His use of Google Classroom to share video links, project details, and resources extends learning beyond the STAC and supports students in accessing information independently. Mr. Den Haan models effective use of technology for students, demonstrating how digital tools can support research, planning, and communication. His integration of both traditional shop technologies and modern digital tools prepares students for a range of future pathways.

## DOMAIN: Leadership and Community

### Competency: Teachers collaborate with other teachers and school colleagues to create and sustain learning communities in their classrooms and in their schools.

Mr. Den Haan actively collaborates with colleagues to create and sustain learning communities at Ross Drive and beyond. He works in conjunction with Harold Brathwaite Secondary School teachers and administration to further the school's participation in the annual UNSDG event, attending as supervisor, mentor, and judge. His organization of the Grade 4-5 field trip to the Louise Arbour Secondary School STEAM Challenge and his accompaniment of Grade 5-8 students to the annual Peel Skills Competition at Sheridan College demonstrate his commitment to providing enriching experiences that connect elementary students to secondary and post-secondary opportunities.

Within the school, Mr. Den Haan works with colleagues to address student and classroom concerns, sharing positive moments and offering solutions to behavioural challenges. He contributes to school culture through his involvement in the Cancer research fundraising drive and Social Club. His willingness to coach and mentor student teachers and new colleagues, as well as his role coaching junior and intermediate sports teams, reflect his dedication to fostering teamwork, fair play, and growth mindset among students and adults alike.

### Competency: Teachers work with professionals, parents, and members of the community to enhance pupil learning, pupil achievement, and school programs.

Mr. Den Haan works collaboratively with professionals, parents, and community members to enhance student learning and school programs. His leadership in organizing field experiences—such as the LASS STEAM Challenge for ELL students—demonstrates his commitment to providing equitable access to enriching opportunities. These experiences were acknowledged by the school's ELL lead teacher and provided meaningful engagement for students who may benefit from additional support.

He reaches out to colleagues to highlight examples of student excellence in the STAC, ensuring that student success in this specialized area is recognized and celebrated. His communication with classroom teachers regarding students of concern—whether to share positive moments or discuss solutions—strengthens the support network for all learners. Through his involvement in school-wide initiatives and his role in connecting students to community resources, Mr. Den Haan enhances learning opportunities and contributes to a culture of shared responsibility for student success.
