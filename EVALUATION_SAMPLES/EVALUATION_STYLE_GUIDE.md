# Teacher Evaluation Style Guide

## Document Structure

**Format:** Appendix B - Summative Report Form for Experienced Teachers (Ontario Regulation 99/02)

### Header Section
- **Teacher Info:** Last Name, First Name, Employee Number
- **Appraiser Info:** Last Name, First Name
- **School/Organization:** School Name, Board Name
- **Assignment Description:** Brief description of grade/subject, full-time/part-time status
- **Meeting/Observation Dates:** Pre-observation, Classroom Observation, Post-observation (yyyy/mm/dd format)
- **Focus of Observation:** Specific competencies or "all competencies"

### Main Body Structure (5 Domains)

Each domain follows this pattern:
1. **Domain Header** - Bold text with domain name
2. **Competency Bullet Points** - 4 competencies (Commitment), 4 (Professional Knowledge), 5 (Professional Practice), 2 (Leadership), 1 (Ongoing Learning)
3. **Narrative Comments** - Detailed paragraph(s) addressing the competencies

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone & Voice
- **Professional and formal** - Use complete sentences, academic language
- **Specific and evidence-based** - Reference actual observations, student behaviors, classroom practices
- **Supportive yet evaluative** - Balance praise with constructive acknowledgment of practice
- **Aligned with board priorities** - Reference PDSB (Peel District School Board) initiatives where applicable:
  - Black Student Success Strategy
  - Culturally Responsive and Relevant Pedagogy (CRRP)
  - Science of Reading
  - Equity and anti-racism
  - Social-Emotional Learning (SEL)
  - SIEP (School Improvement and Equity Plan)

### Key Phrases & Terminology

**Commitment Domain:**
- "demonstrates a strong commitment to the well-being and development of all pupils"
- "fosters respect, empathy, and inclusivity"
- "validates student feelings"
- "promotes equity and anti-bias practices"
- "culturally responsive texts"
- "establishes a warm and predictable structure"
- "active and the students were clearly engaged"

**Professional Knowledge Domain:**
- "demonstrates mastery/thorough understanding of the Ontario curriculum"
- "integrates research-based strategies"
- "Science of Reading practices"
- "Universal Design for Learning (UDL) principles"
- "culturally responsive pedagogy"
- "diagnostic assessments, progress monitoring, exit tickets"
- "evidence-based approaches"

**Professional Practice Domain:**
- "creates meaningful learning experiences"
- "co-constructs classroom expectations"
- "clear success criteria"
- "whole-group, small-group, and one-on-one teaching"
- "communicates effectively with parents through [specific methods]"
- "ongoing assessment practices"
- "modifies programs to meet diverse needs"
- "accommodations and IEP goals"

**Leadership Domain:**
- "actively collaborates with colleagues"
- "shares resources"
- "participates in problem-solving discussions"
- "contributes to school-wide initiatives"
- "mentorship of new teachers"

**Ongoing Learning Domain:**
- "demonstrates a commitment to continuous improvement"
- "regularly reflects on practice"
- "applies evidence-based approaches"
- "engagement in professional development"
- "AQ courses" (Additional Qualifications)

### Sentence Structure Patterns

**Opening statements typically begin with:**
- "[Teacher Name] demonstrates..."
- "[Teacher Name] is..."
- "[Teacher Name] actively..."
- "Observations show..."
- "This was clearly evident..."

**Evidence integration:**
- "During the observation, it was evident that..."
- "Through conversations, classroom visits and the observation..."
- "His/Her [specific practice] was clearly evident throughout..."
- "This is evident through..."

**Alignment statements:**
- "These practices reflect the PDSB's emphasis on..."
- "This approach is aligned with..."
- "His/Her alignment with board priorities..."

### Competency Organization

Each domain narrative should:
1. **Open with broad statement** about overall performance in the domain
2. **Provide specific examples** from observations and practice
3. **Connect to board priorities/initiatives**
4. **Reference student outcomes/behaviors**
5. **Mention specific programs/tools used** (UFLI, Jump Math, etc.)

## Overall Rating Section

**Structure:**
1. Check "Satisfactory" box (unless unsatisfactory - then note Improvement Plan)
2. **Comments on Overall Rating:** Summarize strengths across all domains
3. **Professional Growth Goals:** 2-3 specific, actionable recommendations tied to board priorities

**Overall Comments Template:**
"[Teacher Name] is a [adjective: committed/reflective/highly dedicated] teacher. His/Her practice is grounded in [key strengths: strong relationships/effective instructional strategies/deep understanding of equity]. While there is always room for continued refinement—as is true for all educators—he/she demonstrates consistently strong performance across all competency areas. His/Her alignment with board priorities, particularly around [specific priorities], is evident and appreciated."

## Growth Goals Template

Goals should be:
- **Specific and actionable**
- **Aligned with board priorities**
- **Focused on professional growth**

**Examples:**
- "Continue integrating CRRP into planning and instruction"
- "Continue to refine practice around the Science of Reading"
- "Build on existing collaboration by taking on a formal leadership role in [area]"
- "Continue engaging in professional learning around [specific topic]"

## Formatting Notes

- Use formal educator titles (Mr./Mrs./Ms. + Last Name) consistently
- Dates in yyyy/mm/dd format
- Refer to Peel District School Board (PDSB) or specific organization
- Include reference to Education Act and Ontario Regulation 99/02
- Signature lines: Principal and Teacher with dates

## Sample Competency Narrative Length

- **Commitment:** 1-2 substantial paragraphs (8-12 sentences)
- **Professional Knowledge:** 1-2 substantial paragraphs
- **Professional Practice:** 2-3 substantial paragraphs
- **Leadership:** 1-2 paragraphs
- **Ongoing Learning:** 1 paragraph

Total length: 3-5 pages of narrative text

## Quality Indicators

Strong evaluations include:
- ✅ Specific observed behaviors (not just general statements)
- ✅ Connection to board strategic plans
- ✅ Reference to student engagement/learning
- ✅ Mention of specific tools/programs used
- ✅ Evidence of differentiation/modifications
- ✅ Equity and inclusion language
- ✅ Parent/family communication examples
- ✅ Collaboration with colleagues/support staff
