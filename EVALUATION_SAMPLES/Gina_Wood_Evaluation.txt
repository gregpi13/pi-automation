DOMAIN: Commitment to Pupils and Pupil Learning

Competency: Teachers demonstrate commitment to the well-being and development of all pupils.

Ms. Wood demonstrates a strong commitment to student well-being and inclusive practice. She prioritizes making assessments accessible to all students, particularly those with diverse learning needs. This involves providing accommodations such as extended time, alternative formats, or breaking tasks into smaller, manageable steps to support student confidence and focus. She works to build respectful and trusting relationships with her students, creating a space where they feel valued, comfortable, and encouraged to actively participate.

Competency: Teachers are dedicated in their efforts to teach and support pupil learning and achievement.

Ms. Wood supports learners in practicing new skills by providing opportunities for guided practice, helping them build confidence and master concepts step by step. For instance, when teaching simple essay writing, each step was monitored and conferences occurred to ensure success. She aims to strike a balance between student-led and teacher-directed discussions, allowing students to explore ideas on their own while benefiting from clear guidance. A good example of this balance emerged when examining residential schools; after discussing what students knew, her class explored the United Nations Rights of a Child in small groups and returned as a larger class to discuss the inconsistencies they found.

Competency: Teachers treat all pupils equitably and with respect.

Ms. Wood demonstrates care and respect for students by maintaining positive interactions, promoting polite and respectful student interactions, and addressing inappropriate student behaviour in a positive manner. She communicates information from a bias-free, multicultural perspective and ensures and models bias-free assessment to address equality.

Competency: Teachers provide an environment for learning that encourages pupils to be problem-solvers, decision-makers, lifelong-learners, and contributing members of a changing society.

Ms. Wood provides students with appropriate opportunities for independent practice of new skills and employs effective questioning techniques that encourage higher-level thinking skills. She provides guidance and appropriate feedback to learners on attainment of new concepts and skills, and encourages feedback, risk taking, questioning, and experimentation by establishing a non-threatening learning environment. She encourages students to be cognisant of their personal strengths and capabilities with respect to possible career paths.

DOMAIN: Professional Knowledge

Competency: Teachers know their subject matter, the Ontario curriculum, and education-related legislation.

Ms. Wood demonstrates a strong understanding of the Ontario curriculum and stays up to date on any changes to the documents, reexamining lessons accordingly. She explains subject content clearly when teaching. In language arts, for example, she models proper paragraph structure by showing students how to organize ideas with a clear topic sentence, supporting details, and a concluding sentence. She also encourages critical thinking in history by guiding students to analyze events, consider different perspectives, and explain cause-and-effect relationships.

Competency: Teachers know a variety of effective teaching and assessment practices.

Ms. Wood demonstrates effective questioning strategies that promote higher-order thinking and deeper understanding. She asks open-ended questions and prompts students to explain their reasoning. She provides clear guidance and timely, constructive feedback to support learners in developing and mastering new concepts and skills. For example, she models how to break down complex questions into smaller, manageable parts by guiding learners to identify lead-in information, circle the question word, underline the main idea to determine what is being asked, and approach the problem step-by-step.

She consistently provides constructive feedback as part of the evaluation process. Multi-step assignments include required check-ins to ensure students are progressing appropriately before moving forward. Understanding is regularly assessed through both discussions and written work, allowing instruction to be adjusted, concepts to be revisited, and next steps to be effectively planned. Assessment strategies are clearly aligned with learning objectives, and rubrics are shared with students to support transparency and reinforce connections to those objectives.

Competency: Teachers know a variety of effective classroom management strategies.

Ms. Wood fosters an inclusive learning environment by ensuring that all students have equitable opportunities to succeed, for example by differentiating instruction, providing multiple ways for students to demonstrate their learning, and offering additional support based on individual needs. Effective classroom management strategies are consistently applied to promote a respectful and productive atmosphere. Expectations for behaviour are clear, and the established code of conduct is implemented consistently and fairly.

Competency: Teachers know how pupils learn and the factors that influence pupil learning and achievement.

Ms. Wood takes into account different learning styles when selecting materials and media, using a mix of visuals, hands-on activities, discussions, and written tasks to support all learners. She adapts instruction through flexible grouping, such as working with small groups for targeted support, pairing students for peer learning, or providing one-on-one guidance when needed. For example, she may group students based on readiness for a task, then adjust groups as understanding develops to ensure everyone is supported and challenged.

DOMAIN: Teaching Practice

Competency: Teachers use their professional knowledge and understanding of pupils, curriculum, legislation, teaching practices, and classroom management strategies to promote the learning and achievement of their pupils.

Ms. Wood uses instructional time in a focused and purposeful way; lessons are well-paced and aligned with learning goals. She organizes subject matter into clear, meaningful lessons by breaking concepts into manageable steps and connecting them to prior knowledge. She supports students in developing skills to critically assess information by modeling how to evaluate sources, ask questions, and identify key ideas. For example, she guides students through analyzing images in history to understand bias and explain their thinking with evidence.

Competency: Teachers communicate effectively with pupils, parents, and colleagues.

Ms. Wood follows school/board guidelines on reporting with diligence, conducts teacher-student conferences, and communicates clear, challenging, and achievable expectations to and for students.

Competency: Teachers conduct ongoing assessment of their pupils' progress, evaluate their achievement, and report results to pupils and parents regularly.

Ms. Wood uses ongoing reporting to keep both students and parents informed and to chart student progress. She employs a variety of appropriate assessment and evaluation techniques to ensure comprehensive understanding of student learning.

Competency: Teachers adapt and refine their teaching practices through continuous learning and reflection, using a variety of sources and resources.

Ms. Wood assesses and reviews program delivery for relevance and uses provincial achievement standards and competency statements as a reference point for evaluation of teaching.

Competency: Teachers use appropriate technology in their teaching practices and related professional responsibilities.

Ms. Wood models and promotes effective use of technology to promote student learning.

DOMAIN: Leadership and Community

Competency: Teachers collaborate with other teachers and school colleagues to create and sustain learning communities in their classrooms and in their schools.

Ms. Wood learns with and from colleagues and others in communities of learners. She participates as an effective team member and shares expertise with others, for example by acting as mentor, peer coach, or associate teacher. She participates effectively by contributing to grade, division, and/or subject teams.

Competency: Teachers work with professionals, parents, and members of the community to enhance pupil learning, pupil achievement, and school programs.

Ms. Wood engages others effectively through shared problem solving and conflict resolution. She reaches out to parents, inviting them to share their knowledge and skills in supporting their child's academic and emotional growth.

Comments on Overall Rating

Ms. Wood demonstrates consistently strong performance across all competency areas. Her practice reflects a solid commitment to student well-being, equitable assessment practices, and inclusive instruction. She effectively balances student-led and teacher-directed learning, as evidenced in her approach to complex topics like residential schools and the UN Rights of a Child.

Her strength in assessment is particularly noteworthy; she consistently provides constructive feedback, uses rubrics to support transparency, and aligns evaluation with learning objectives. Her questioning strategies promote higher-order thinking, and her flexible grouping practices ensure all learners receive appropriate support and challenge.

Ms. Wood's collaborative approach and ongoing professional learning demonstrate her commitment to continuous improvement. She actively seeks input from colleagues, observes best practices, and applies new learning to enhance her teaching.

While there is always room for continued refinement—as is true for all educators—Ms. Wood demonstrates consistently strong performance across all competency areas.
