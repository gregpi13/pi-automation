Domain: Commitment to Pupils and Pupil Learning
Competency: Teachers demonstrate commitment to the well-being and development of all pupils. 
I focus on getting to know my students and fostering their holistic growth: physical, emotional, social, and cognitive. 
Questionnaire sent to parents early in the year regarding learning styles, preferred workplace environment, interests, hobbies, extra-curriculars, etc.  
Encourage students to take care of their mental health by getting a good sleep and eating a healthy breakfast/allow students to eat their breakfast/snacks throughout the day 
Understanding that their emotional state strongly impacts their day, and making sure to check in with students regarding their feelings in the mornings
I ensure my classroom is culturally responsive to all learners in the room.  
Differentiate instruction in the classroom with the use of procedural choice and product choice in assignments  
Use the gradual release of learning method and provide students with mentor texts for reading and writing 
Vocabulary pre-teaching is used in subject areas, as well as continual reinforcement of vocabulary (e.g., subject-specific vocabulary in Science/Social Studies, Math) using models (e.g., Freyer model)
Texts highlighting differing identities are incorporated in the classroom (e.g., Muslim girls in sports, Black Boy Joy)
I demonstrate care and respect for students by maintaining positive interactions
I greet students by there name and with a smile when they enter the classroom. 
I praise students for their hard work and effort in class emphasizing a growth mindset 
I praise students for trying their best and persevering through difficult tasks
I remind students that making mistakes is a part of the learning process 
I address student behavior and interactions by reminding students of the class values and why we have these values a class. 
I encourage students to engage in reflection in regards to how their behavior impacts others 
I appreciate students for their effort and making good choices in the classroom

Domain: Commitment to Pupils and Pupil Learning
Competency: Teachers treat all pupils equitably and with respect. 
I maintain positive relationships with students by celebrating their unique identities.
Private conversations are conducted in the hallways so students don’t feel put on the spot and feel respected
Non-verbal cues are used to communicate with students who require multiple reminders, so that they don’t feel called out in front of their peers (e.g., hand gestures)
Awareness of, respect towards, and appreciation of different identities and cultures across the room (e.g., use of pronouns, accommodations for students fasting, showing interest in Arabic school, etc.) 
Encouragement of students to be themselves in the classroom (e.g., students sharing their languages, patois, Urdu, Tamil, ect). 
2) I provide students with tiered supports according to their individual needs.
Flexible grouping, grouping based on level, interest, and personalities 
Seating accommodations (e.g.,  in proximity to the teacher)

Exposure to independent tasks, partnered tasks, and group tasks
Opportunities to share learning one-on-one, in conversations, in think-pair-share
Gradual release of responsibility model  

Domain: Commitment to Pupils and Pupil Learning
Competency: Teachers provide an environment for learning that encourages pupils to be problem-solvers, decision-makers, lifelong learners, and contributing members of a changing society. 
I encourage students to make connections between learning in class and home/the real world. 
During Math lessons, students are encouraged to learn from and involve their parents in the learning process (e.g., ask your parents about the provincial and federal taxes and how they learned multiplication).
Students are encouraged to make connections between learning and the real world (e.g., estimate the total cost of groceries before purchasing) 
Students evaluated non-renewable energy sources and discussed how these sources can respond to global issues 
I encourage learners to be problem solvers and to pursue their interests through the inquiry process. 
Students are asked to complete weekly homework regarding the Waterloo Problems of the Week 
 and communication was sent home to parents about the importance of these weekly problems 
Students created a project on the human body, which they planned, built, and created in the maker space. Students had to respond to a design challenge, which included having a moving part and showing how the system worked. 
Students are encouraged to pursue independent research inquiries outside of the curriculum (e.g., Slideshow on diseases)

Domain: Professional Knowledge 
Competency: Teachers know a variety of effective classroom management strategies.  
I create a consistent and predictable classroom environment and adjust classroom management supports according to individual student needs. 
Classroom expectations are posted and reinforced when necessary. 
Class schedules are posted and reviewed in the mornings.
Changes to the routine are communicated early. 
I use nonverbal communication to redirect specific students (e.g., hand motions, a light touch on the shoulder, eye contact, proximity).
Certain students require additional movement breaks, brain breaks, and sometimes a complete reset and so breaks are provided (e.g., please take this to the office) 
Alignment of behaviours with identities/growth mindset/ the ability to make good choices/explanation of consequences. 
2) Fostering a positive and collaborative culture between students and proactively addressing issues, emphasizing restorative practices. 
Students are praised when exhibiting positive behaviours in the classroom (e.g., getting out work materials right away)
Students are praised when demonstrating behaviours that align with class values (e.g., kindness, helping their classmates)
I support students in the hallway by openly resolving recess incidents between peers, using the restorative method (e.g., encouraging students to use I feel statements)
Ongoing monthly community circles to teach/reinforce school values (e.g., anti-bullying) 
Balance between points awarded to groups of students and individuals to encourage a collaborative approach to reaching classroom goals

Domain: Teaching Practice  
Competency: Teachers conduct ongoing assessments of their pupils’ progress, evaluate their achievement, and report results to pupils and parents regularly.
 Uses a variety of appropriate assessment and evaluation techniques.
Assessment for, of, and as learning (e.g., rubrics, checklists, self-assessments)
Embedding process and product differentiation across assignments (e.g., choice in terms of how to present the final product)
Checkbricks, rubrics, tests,  anecdotal records, formative feedback on the spot 
Success criteria are proactively shared with students, and students take part in the co-creation of success criteria
Check in with students on the spot (e.g., thumbs up, thumbs down, etc., show me how many fingers, hold up your whiteboards, tell me one thing you learned before leaving for lunch)
Engages in meaningful dialogue with students to provide feedback during the teaching/learning process.
Provide students with positive feedback and next steps on the spot before submitting the final product    
Balance of open-ended questions and tiered support in terms of supporting student responses (e.g., vocabulary, prompt choices of possible responses) 
Provide on-the-spot feedback during oral presentations, emphasizing what the student did well before suggesting next steps
I always check over their work and 9/10 times send them back with one additional thing to improve. But I start to scale back on this as the year progresses, as with enough modelling of being reflective, students are doing this more and more before handing in their work to me.
